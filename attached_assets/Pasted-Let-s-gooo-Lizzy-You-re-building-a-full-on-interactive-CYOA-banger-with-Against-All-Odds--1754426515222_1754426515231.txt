## 🎮 **AGAINST ALL ODDS – FULL STORY STRUCTURE**

---

### **SCREEN 1 – Title Screen**

**Text:**
**AGAINST ALL ODDS**
Press **Begin Story** to start Khalil’s journey.

---

### **SCREEN 2 – Meet Khalil**

**Narration:**
Meet **Khalil** — 17, smart, underestimated, raised in the hood.
Dad’s in prison for a crime he didn’t commit.
Mom’s holding it down with three younger siblings: **Kya (13), King and Kayson (5-year-old twins)**.
Khalil works nights at a local diner to help out — but his dream?
**Law school.**
People laugh when he says it, but he’s not backing down.

---

### **SCREEN 3 – The Debate Team Offer**

**Narration:**
Khalil’s in class, zoning out when the teacher calls on him.
He answers flawlessly.
“Why don’t you try out for the debate team?” the teacher says. “Tryouts are Tuesday after school.”
He shrugs, unsure.

---

### **SCREEN 4 – Talk with Family**

**Narration:**
That night, Khalil tells his mom.
She’s proud — tears in her eyes.
He visits his dad and shares the news.
“Take it,” his dad says. “You were born for this.”

---

### **SCREEN 5 – Late-Night Call**

**Narration:**
10:03 PM.
Khalil’s boss calls:
“Hey Khalil, can you take a shift Tuesday? Someone called in sick.”
Debate tryouts or shift?

**Choices:**

* **Go to work** (gain Respect, lose Knowledge)
* **Go to debate** (gain Knowledge, lose Money)
* **Try to do both** (risky — 50/50 shot: gain both or fail both)

---

### **SCREEN 6 – Jamal’s Party Invite**

**Narration:**
Monday after school, Khalil’s friend **Jamal** pulls up.
“Yo, party tonight at 7! You in?”

**Choices:**

* **Say no, I gotta study** (gain Knowledge +10)
* **Say yes, let’s go** (lose Respect -10)
* **Make an excuse — baby sibling is sick** (no change)

---

### **SCREEN 7 – First Debate Day**

**Narration:**
If Khalil chose debate, he’s standing in front of a small crowd.
His palms sweat.
But then…
He speaks.
Clear. Confident. Bold.
Applause.

**Automatic boost:**

* Knowledge
* Confidence

---

### **SCREEN 8 – Missing Homework**

**Narration:**
Next day, teacher says, “Where’s your paper?”
Khalil forgot.
He stayed up babysitting.
He can explain or take the L.

**Choices:**

* **Be honest — I forgot** (gain Respect, lose Knowledge)
* **Make up a lie** (no penalty, but lowers trust for future scenes)
* **Beg for more time** (chance to resubmit, but boss cuts a shift)

---

### **SCREEN 9 – The Customer Incident**

**Narration:**
At the diner, a customer talks reckless.
“You people are all the same.”
Khalil feels heated.
His boss watches silently.

**Choices:**

* **Keep calm, finish the shift** (gain Respect)
* **Clap back** (lose Respect, gain Mental Strength)
* **Walk out** (lose Money, boost confidence)

---

### **SCREEN 10 – A Letter from His Dad**

**Narration:**
A letter arrives from prison.
His dad shares a quote:
*"They told me I’d never make it. So I had to."*
Khalil pins it on his wall.
(+ Mental Strength)

---

### **SCREEN 11 – A Classmate’s Offer**

**Narration:**
At school, a known plug offers Khalil money to “drop off a package.”
“No risk, easy cash.”

**Choices:**

* **Say no, I’m not risking it** (gain Respect)
* **Say yes** (quick Money boost, but future scene risk)
* **Walk away silently** (neutral)

---

### **SCREEN 12 – A Teacher’s Recommendation**

**Narration:**
Khalil’s teacher says she wants to recommend him for a pre-law summer program.
He needs references, an essay, and… time.

**Choices:**

* **Go for it full force** (requires high Knowledge to succeed)
* **Turn it down — too much right now**
* **Say yes, but half-commit** (random chance of success/failure)

---

### **SCREEN 13 – Family Emergency**

**Narration:**
One twin has a fever.
Mom has to work.
Khalil has a midterm tomorrow.

**Choices:**

* **Stay home, skip the test** (gain Respect, lose Knowledge)
* **Leave siblings with Kya** (risk — if Kya fails, you lose Respect)
* **Call in backup (auntie/neighbor)** (safe, but uses a favor)

---

### **SCREEN 14 – The Interview**

**Narration:**
Khalil gets called in for a short scholarship interview.
He’s nervous.

**Question:**
Why do you want to be a lawyer?

**Player types or selects:**

* “To fight for people like my dad.”
* “Because I want to prove them wrong.”
* “I want a better life.”

Points shift based on choice — all valid, but affect tone of future scenes.

---

### **SCREEN 15 – Jamal Again**

**Narration:**
Jamal pulls up again:
“Bro, you changing. You too good for us now?”
Khalil has to respond.

**Choices:**

* **“I still ride for y’all, but I got goals.”** (gain Respect)
* **“Man, I don’t need this.”** (gain Mental Strength)
* **Say nothing.** (no change)

---

### **SCREEN 16 – Dad’s Court Hearing**

**Narration:**
Big day. Khalil has a chance to speak for his dad.
Eyes on him.
Will he?

**Choices:**

* **Speak boldly** (big Respect + Confidence)
* **Let the lawyer handle it** (no change)
* **Freeze up** (lose Respect, but opens future redemption path)

---

### **SCREEN 17 – Final Debate Tournament**

**Narration:**
Khalil’s team makes finals.
He’s up against a private school kid who mocks his background.

**Choices:**

* **Stay cool, use logic** (win)
* **Call him out, street-style** (points for realness, risk losing match)
* **Fumble it** (still get a life lesson)

---

### **SCREEN 18 – Program Results**

**Narration:**
The email hits.
Did he get the pre-law summer program?

**If Knowledge + Respect are high → ACCEPTED.**
**If low → REJECTED.**
**If middle → WAITLISTED, but hope remains.**

---

**Narration:**
Khalil’s applying to college.
No longer the busboy.
Still the big brother.
Still hungry.
Still climbing.
